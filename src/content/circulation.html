Rhythms And Drugs

What the addon adds on top of ACM's arrest handling: perfusing rhythms, reversible causes, and drugs that stop working when you lean on them.

The one thing

A {{pressor|pressor}} cannot manufacture volume. Every drug here buys time against a problem you still have to fix, and most buy less each time you reach for them.

01

Perfusing Rhythms

ACM's amiodarone is arrest only. This adds rhythm control in a casualty who still has a pulse.

RhythmConverts toAmiodarone effect needed
Atrial fibrillation with RVRRate controlled0.40
Atrial tachycardiaSinus0.40
SVTSinus0.70

SVT sits at a higher bar because adenosine is first line, and amiodarone should feel like the second choice it is.

Why torsades is excludedDeliberate

Amiodarone prolongs the QT interval, which is the mechanism that causes torsades in the first place, so letting it treat torsades would train exactly the wrong reflex.

The cumulative 2200 mg ceiling models the same concern from the other direction: keep pushing and you are building toward the problem rather than away from it.

02

Push Dose Pressor

A bridge to buy you working time. Each push returns less than the last.

DoseResponseMAP support
1100%14.0 mmHg
272%10.1
352%7.3
437%5.2
619%2.7

Responsiveness recovers on a half life of 420 seconds, so a casualty who genuinely stabilises gets it back. From four doses deep: 52% after seven minutes, 72% after fourteen.

By the fifth push the drug is telling you something. The answer is usually blood.

Why this matters more than the capDesign

There was already a ceiling on stacked support. A ceiling on its own reads as an invitation to keep pushing until you hit it.

Diminishing returns work better. Receptors downregulate, and the underlying problem carries on while you are treating the number, so the drug getting weaker is the model pointing you back at the cause.

03

Reversible Causes

ACM's ROSC gate is extended rather than replaced. The addon can only ever make ROSC harder, never easier, and only when ACMI_hcEff_rhythm is enabled.

What it adds is the Hs and Ts that ACM does not check: hypoxia, hypovolaemia, tension pneumothorax, acidosis. A casualty in arrest from an uncorrected cause will not come back until you correct it, which is the entire point of the mnemonic.

Oxygen delivery has no part in the ROSC gate. Nothing in the delivery model can quietly make a casualty unrecoverable.

Red Flags

  • Reaching for push dose a fifth time. You are getting a fifth of the first dose. Find the cause.
  • Amiodarone for a wide complex you have not identified. If it is torsades you are making it worse.
  • Working an arrest without checking reversible causes. ROSC will not come until the cause is fixed.
  • Pressors in an acidotic casualty. Support is blunted by up to 45%, so correcting the acidosis does more than another dose will.
