Oxygen Delivery

The number that decides whether a casualty is dying. There is no readout for it anywhere, on purpose.

The one thing

Saturation tells you what fraction of the haemoglobin is loaded, and nothing about how much haemoglobin there is. A casualty can read 96% while delivering almost nothing.

01

What It Models

Delivery is haemoglobin concentration times saturation times cardiac output, expressed as a fraction of normal. All three terms move independently, which is why it catches cases saturation alone cannot.

StateDeliveryWhat saturation shows
Healthy1.01Normal

Class III haemorrhage0.58Often still normal

Class III plus whole blood1.10Normal
Pure hypoxia0.89Low, obviously
Deep shock0.35May still look survivable

Read the middle rows again. Crystalloid raises delivery less than blood does, and the display shows the same number for both.

02

Detail

Why stroke volume falls faster than volumeFrank-Starling

A linear preload term produces nonsense at the bottom end, because the Frank-Starling curve is steep in exactly the region a shocked casualty is sitting in. Losing preload costs stroke volume faster than proportionally.

The model uses an exponent above one for this, which is why the gap between class II and class III is much larger than the difference in blood volume suggests.

Why crystalloid fadesDilution

Crystalloid raises volume without adding carriers, so it dilutes haemoglobin. Delivery improves because output improves, but less than blood would, and the dilution decays with a half-life of twenty minutes as fluid leaves the intravascular space.

Plasma is not decayed, because it is a colloid and stays where you put it.

Why waking up sticksThree guards

Low delivery drives acidosis, and without protection a casualty could be woken and then immediately pushed back under by debt accrued while they were down. Three guards prevent it.

An awake casualty saturating above the consciousness floor never accrues debt at all. Entry and clearing thresholds are separated so the state does not chatter at the boundary. And a reperfusion window after ROSC gives circulation time to catch up before anything is charged.

A ventilated casualty with a secured airway and good saturations counts as winning in the same way an awake one does.

03

What It Drives

ConsumerEffect
Metabolic acidosisAccrues below the critical fraction, faster toward lethal
Head injurySecondary hypoxic insult reads delivery, not saturation
Pressor supportAcidosis blunts it by up to 45%

Acidosis blunts pressor support only. It does not touch consciousness, and delivery has no part in the ROSC gate, so nothing here can quietly make a casualty unrecoverable.

Red Flags

  • Trusting a good saturation in a bleeding casualty. The failure this whole system exists to teach.
  • Filling with crystalloid and moving on. It buys cardiac output without adding carriers, and the benefit fades.
  • Ventilating without filling. Positive pressure costs preload, which costs output, which costs delivery.
