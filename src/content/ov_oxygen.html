PILLAR // ADVANCED FUNCTIONS

Oxygen
And Saturation

Three systems can write a saturation number and only one of them may. This page is the layer separation that keeps ACM Extended from fighting ACE, and it is the most important thing here for anyone extending the addon.

The one thing

Exactly one function may own saturation at a time. Two writers do not average, they alternate, and the number visibly flickers between them four times a second.

01

Three Layers: ace_medical_spo2, TargetVitals & getSpO2

There are three layers involved and they are easy to confuse because all three end up affecting a number labelled SpO2.

LayerVariableDriven by
ACE simulationace_medical_spo2Ventilation and gas exchange
ACM targetACM_core_TargetVitals_OxygenSaturationWhat the patient should read
ACM displaygetSpO2Read-time transform, adds blood loss

ACE behaviour. ace_medical_spo2 is driven by ventilation and gas exchange, with no blood volume term at all.

ACM behaviour. Blood loss enters at the ACM getSpO2 display layer as a read-time transform, not as a stored value.

Override. ACM Extended writes ACM_core_TargetVitals_OxygenSaturation, the ACM target layer, and never touches ace_medical_spo2. That is the entire reason there is no fight with ACE.

Why writing the ACE variable failsRead before extending

ACE's vitals cycle recomputes ace_medical_spo2 from its own gas exchange model on every pass. Anything you write there is overwritten on the next tick.

The failure mode is not a clean revert. Your value holds for a fraction of a second, ACE's value replaces it, yours is written again, and the displayed number oscillates between the two. On a pulse oximeter that reads as a value that will not settle, which players correctly report as a bug.

Writing the ACM target layer instead means ACE's simulation continues undisturbed and ACM resolves the target on read. Nothing is fighting because nothing is being contradicted.

The single-writer guardCopy this pattern

Several ACM Extended systems have a legitimate claim on saturation: the ventilator shunt model, the altitude model, and the megacode scenario driver. They cannot all write it.

The rule is that the ventilator owns saturation whenever it is driving, because its shunt model already folds altitude in through the same ratio. Every other writer stands down. In fn_altitudeTick that is one line:

if (_patient getVariable ["ACMI_vent_driving", false]) exitWith {};

Any new function that writes saturation must carry the same guard. Without it the symptom is a saturation that jumps between two plausible values while a ventilated casualty is being carried uphill, which is close to impossible to diagnose from the outside.

02

Breathing State Multipliers

ACM behaviour. getBreathingState returns an effectiveness figure knocked down by pneumothorax, tension pneumothorax, hemothorax and CBRN exposure. Each insult multiplies.

Override. The model is reproduced verbatim, then a graded over-resuscitation pulmonary edema penalty is applied on top, multiplicatively, exactly like the hemothorax term it sits beside.

InsultEffect on breathing state
Hemothorax fluid above 0.3scales to 0.1x at 1.5

Why edema is not an SpO2 capDesign

The first implementation pinned saturation to a floor when the casualty was fluid overloaded. It was replaced, and the reasoning is worth keeping.

A cap is a display lie. It forces a number without changing the physiology behind it, so everything downstream of saturation carries on as though the lungs were fine, and the number snaps rather than moving.

Degrading breathing effectiveness instead means fluid overload floods the alveoli and damages gas exchange the same way a pneumothorax does, and ACM's own oxygen simulation carries saturation down from there on its own curve. The result moves gradually, responds to the things that should help it, and cannot be walked around by anything reading saturation directly.

The penalty scales from nothing at the onset threshold to its maximum at the overload cap, and has its own coefficient so it can be tuned without disturbing any other breathing insult.

03

Where DO2 Is Computed

Delivery is haemoglobin concentration times saturation times {{cardiac-output|cardiac output}}, expressed as a fraction of normal. The clinical table of what each shock state delivers lives on the Oxygen Delivery (DO2) system page and is not repeated here.

What matters at this level is that all three terms are read fresh each tick rather than cached, and that none of them is derived from the others. Haemoglobin comes from blood volume, saturation from the ACM target layer described above, and cardiac output from stroke volume times rate. A change in any one moves delivery without touching the other two.

That independence is the reason the model catches cases a saturation reading cannot. A casualty can hold a normal saturation while haemoglobin halves, because saturation reports the fraction of what is present that is loaded and has no term for how much is present.
