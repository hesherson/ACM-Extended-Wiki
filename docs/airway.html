Airway And Chest

Decompression, seals, drainage and tubes. The part of the addon where doing the wrong thing confidently causes the injury you were treating for.

The one thing

A needle costs something. Decompressing a chest that did not need it seeds the pneumothorax you were looking for, and repeating it raises the chance of turning that into a tension.

01

Needle Decompression

The action is available on any living casualty at any time, rather than only when a tension is present, so the decision comes from what you find on examination rather than from a label the game has already applied.

Before the needle goes in, the indication is checked. If a tension or simple pneumothorax is present, it works as expected. If not, you have just made a hole in a healthy chest.

Set ACMI_ncd_iatrogenicTension to false to disable the tension escalation while keeping the rest.

Why this is in the addonTeaching intent

Most medical sims make needle decompression a free action, which trains the habit of needling anything that looks unwell. That habit is actively dangerous, and it is one of the more common criticisms of prehospital chest decompression in the real literature.

Making it cost something turns it into a decision. You are meant to examine first, and the delay before the iatrogenic pneumothorax declares itself means you find out you were wrong later, which is exactly how it happens.

It is recoverable. A simple pneumothorax you caused can be decompressed and drained like any other, so the cost is time and a lesson rather than a dead casualty.

02

Positive Pressure And Preload

Ventilating a casualty raises intrathoracic pressure, which opposes venous return. In a full tank that costs nothing measurable. In an empty one it costs a great deal.

Volume stateStroke volume penalty
Fullabout 0%
Class III haemorrhage15 to 19%
Maximum modelled35%

High PEEP multiplies it up to 1.5 times. This applies to both the ventilator and manual bagging, so an enthusiastic BVM technique on a shocked casualty does real harm.

The practical rule: fill them before you tube them, and if you must ventilate an empty casualty, keep pressures and PEEP low until volume is restored.

03

Junctional Wounds

Five states, each a distinct step: open, packed, wrapped, and XStat. Bleeding falls as you progress. The AAJT-S applies with a pause, and Combat Gauze is tracked separately.

Red Flags

  • Needling on suspicion alone. Examine first. A wrong needle creates the injury.
  • Repeat needles when the first did not help. Each one raises the tension chance cumulatively.
  • Ventilating an empty casualty. You are trading oxygenation for cardiac output.
  • High PEEP in shock. Multiplies the preload penalty by half again.
