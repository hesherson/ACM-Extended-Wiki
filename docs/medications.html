Medications

Every drug the addon can deliver, with what it does and what it does differently here. Doses shown are the therapeutic dose the registry uses.

Reading these

The clinical detail is standard prehospital pharmacology. The In this addon dropdown on each card is where the modelled behaviour lives: the interactions, the traps, and anything that works differently from stock ACM.

01

Infusions Versus Pushes

Anything given as an infusion runs through the roller clamp and ramps in over about 45 seconds, decaying over 120 seconds after the line stops. Pushes act on ACM's own onset curve.

Several drugs are in the vesicant table, where extravasation causes progressive three stage tissue damage over time rather than an immediate injury. That means a bad line is something you find out about later, so check the site before starting and after any move.

EpinephrineCatecholamine / vasopressor1 mg arrest | 10 mcg push dose
Route
IV, IO, IM, ETT
Onset
1 min IV
Peak
2 to 5 min
Duration
5 to 10 min

Mechanism

Alpha 1 causes vasoconstriction and raises systemic vascular resistance. Beta 1 raises rate and contractility. In arrest the alpha effect is what matters, because it raises coronary and cerebral perfusion pressure during compressions.

Indications

  • Cardiac arrest, any rhythm
  • Anaphylaxis
  • Symptomatic bradycardia unresponsive to atropine
  • Push dose for transient hypotension

Contraindications and cautions

  • No absolute contraindication in arrest
  • Caution in a casualty who is empty, because you are squeezing a tank that has nothing in it
In this addonSpecial considerations

Push dose has tachyphylaxis. Each push returns less than the last, dropping to about a fifth by the sixth dose, and recovering on a half life of 420 seconds. The model is telling you the drug is a bridge and the answer is usually blood. Pressor support also ramps over 45 seconds rather than appearing instantly, and decays over 120 seconds after the line stops. Acidosis blunts the whole effect by up to 45%, so correcting pH does more than another dose.

AmiodaroneClass III antiarrhythmic300 mg arrest | 150 mg over 10 min
Route
IV or IO
Onset
minutes
Peak
variable
Duration
long, cumulative

Mechanism

Blocks potassium channels and prolongs repolarisation and the refractory period. Also has sodium channel, beta and calcium channel activity, which is why it does so many things and has so many side effects.

Indications

  • VF or pulseless VT refractory to defibrillation
  • VT with a pulse
  • Atrial fibrillation with rapid ventricular response
  • SVT after adenosine

Contraindications and cautions

  • Torsades de pointes
  • Known QT prolongation
  • Second or third degree block without pacing
In this addonSpecial considerations

ACM only implements amiodarone for arrest. This addon adds perfusing rhythm control: atrial fibrillation with RVR and atrial tachycardia convert at an effect of 0.40, SVT at 0.70 because adenosine is first line. Torsades is deliberately excluded, since amiodarone prolongs QT and causes it. A cumulative 2200 mg ceiling models the same concern. Arrest dosing is a 300 mg push; with a pulse it is 150 mg over 10 minutes, and faster than 25 mg per minute causes hypotension.

LidocaineClass IB antiarrhythmic / local100 mg, then 2 mg/min
Route
IV, IO, infiltration
Onset
45 to 90 s
Peak
1 to 2 min
Duration
10 to 20 min

Mechanism

Blocks fast sodium channels preferentially in ischaemic tissue, shortening the action potential and suppressing ventricular ectopy.

Indications

  • VF or pulseless VT as an alternative to amiodarone
  • VT with a pulse
  • Local anaesthesia
  • Into an IO before a fluid bolus

Contraindications and cautions

  • Second or third degree block without pacing
  • Known amide anaesthetic allergy
In this addonSpecial considerations

The most detailed drug model here. A therapeutic dose sits at effect 0.6 and holds full effect for 30 minutes, ramping to zero by 90 minutes. While on board it deterministically terminates VT with a pulse and raises defibrillation conversion odds from a floor of 0.2 to certainty at full effect.

There is also a serum level accumulator with a toxicity arc, because the ACM effect value saturates at 1.0 and cannot represent overdose. Plateau in mcg/mL is the drip rate in mg per minute divided by clearance, so 2 mg/min settles around 3.2, which is therapeutic, and 8 mg/min reaches roughly 12.8, which is the seizure band. Half life is compressed to about 5.5 minutes against a real 100 so a careless drip reaches toxicity inside a scenario. Shock reduces clearance to 60% and esmolol to 70%, which raises the plateau and lengthens the half life, so the casualty who most needs the drug clears it worst. Above the seizure threshold an unconscious casualty seizes, and the tell is apnea.

Mechanism

Stabilises the myocardial membrane and is a cofactor in cardiac conduction. In torsades it suppresses the early afterdepolarisations that trigger the arrhythmia.

Indications

  • Torsades de pointes, with or without a pulse
  • Refractory VF where hypomagnesaemia is suspected
  • Severe asthma

Contraindications and cautions

  • Renal failure
  • Known heart block
In this addonSpecial considerations

This is the correct answer for torsades, which is why amiodarone is excluded from it. An effect of 0.35 terminates active torsades and 0.25 suppresses recurrence, both reachable on a drip. If you are looking at a polymorphic wide complex, reach for this rather than the antiarrhythmic you would use for anything else.

Calcium ChlorideElectrolyte1 g
Route
IV or IO, large vein
Onset
immediate
Peak
minutes
Duration
30 to 60 min

Mechanism

Supplies ionised calcium, which is required for the clotting cascade and for myocardial contractility. Chloride salt delivers roughly three times the elemental calcium of gluconate per gram.

Indications

  • Massive transfusion
  • Hyperkalaemia
  • Calcium channel blocker overdose
  • Hypocalcaemia with a low ionised level

Contraindications and cautions

  • Do not run in the same line as bicarbonate or ceftriaxone, it precipitates
In this addonSpecial considerations

Central to the lethal triad model. Stored blood is citrated, and citrate chelates calcium at 0.18 per litre transfused. Below an ionised level of 1.0 you lose clotting, worth up to 1.4 times bleeding, and you lose myocardial function, worth up to 40 mmHg of MAP per unit below threshold. Calcium chloride credits back 0.12 per gram. Give it early in a massive transfusion rather than after things go wrong, because you are racing the citrate rather than treating a number. Gluconate is also stocked at 3 g, which is roughly dose equivalent to 1 g of chloride.

Tranexamic AcidAntifibrinolytic1 g over 10 min
Route
IV or IO
Onset
minutes
Peak
within the hour
Duration
about 8 h

Mechanism

Blocks the lysine binding sites on plasminogen, preventing plasmin from breaking down formed clot. It does not make clot, it stops clot being dismantled.

Indications

  • Haemorrhagic shock within 3 hours of injury
  • Significant traumatic haemorrhage

Contraindications and cautions

  • More than 3 hours from injury, where it increases mortality
  • Known thromboembolic disease
In this addonSpecial considerations

Push it slowly. Given fast it causes hypotension, which is the last thing a bleeding casualty needs. The time window is the teaching point: early it helps and late it harms, so it is a drug that rewards giving it at the point of injury rather than at the aid post.

KetamineDissociative anaesthetic50 mg analgesia | higher for RSI
Route
IV, IO, IM, intranasal
Onset
30 s IV, 3 to 5 min IM
Peak
1 min IV
Duration
10 to 20 min

Mechanism

NMDA receptor antagonist producing dissociation. Preserves airway reflexes and respiratory drive far better than other anaesthetics, and is generally haemodynamically supportive through sympathetic outflow.

Indications

  • Analgesia in trauma
  • Induction for intubation, especially in shock
  • Agitation requiring sedation

Contraindications and cautions

  • Caution in catecholamine depleted casualties, where the sympathomimetic effect is absent and direct myocardial depression shows instead
In this addonSpecial considerations

The addon tracks whether ketamine is on board via fn_ketamineOnBoard, which matters for the sedation and paralysis relationship. A paralysed casualty who is not sedated is a specific failure the model can represent, and ketamine is one of the two agents that answers it. It is the induction agent of choice in a shocked casualty precisely because it does not drop pressure the way propofol does.

Interactions Worth Memorising

  • Amiodarone and torsades. Amiodarone prolongs QT and causes torsades. Magnesium is the answer.
  • Esmolol and lidocaine. Beta blockade cuts lidocaine clearance to 70%, pushing a therapeutic drip toward the seizure band.
  • Shock and lidocaine. Shock cuts clearance to 60%, so the casualty who needs it most clears it worst.
  • Ceftriaxone and calcium. They precipitate. Massive transfusion means calcium, so use a different line.
  • Rocuronium without sedation. Paralysed, aware, in pain, and unable to tell you.
  • Naloxone outlasted by its opioid. They can wake up and go back down. Keep watching.
