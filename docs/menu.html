Medical Menu

Grouped, collapsible, colour coded. Built to keep a long action list scannable under pressure.

Requirement

Grouping needs left-aligned menu text. The markers and indentation that show which group a row belongs to are invisible when text is centred, so the renderer enforces it and the title says so.

01

Structure

Groups expand in place rather than opening a sub page, so you keep your bearings and the row you want stays where you left it.

AirwayGrouped

Flat: Check Airway and Breathing, Inspect Chest, Inspect with Stethoscope.

Groups: Adjuncts (OPA, NPA, i-gel, Intubate, Surgical, Stitch), Ventilation (four BVM oxygen variants plus NRB apply and remove), Chest, Suction, Positioning, Capnography.

AdvancedGrouped

Flat actions, plus an AED group of fifteen entries, plus a Debug group that stays hidden unless debug is enabled.

Examine, Bandage and Medication stay flat. Head Position was folded into Examine rather than kept as its own group.

02

Colour

Each group header carries its own colour and its children are grey, so you can find a section by colour and still read the actions without them competing.

An open group dims to 55% within its own hue, which shows you what is expanded without introducing a second colour to learn.

Why there is no red groupReserved

Red belongs to bleeding and tourniquets on the body image. Using it for a menu section would put the same colour on a warning and on an ordinary navigation header, which costs you the one colour that should always mean the same thing.

The palette was also re-solved by search after a first attempt came out too similar: minimum separation between any two group colours was raised from 0.01 to 0.30, counting hue or luminance, with luminance spread widened from 0.17 to 0.44.

03

Turning It Off

Set ACMI_menuNestEnabled to false for the flat list. Colour headers have their own switch, so you can keep grouping without colour if you prefer.
